Bell X-14: A Leap in Aviation

A Pioneering Aircraft in VTOL Technology

What is the Bell X-14?

The Bell X-14 was a tilt-wing experimental aircraft developed by Bell Aircraft Corporation in the late 1950s. It was one of the first aircraft to demonstrate successful vertical takeoff and landing (VTOL) capabilities, shaping the future of aviation.

Key Milestones

  • 1955: The Bell X-14 program begins as an experimental VTOL aircraft project.
  • 1957: First successful vertical takeoff.
  • 1960: Bell X-14 reaches a top speed of 200 mph during test flights.
  • 1965: The project is retired, but its findings influence aircraft design today.
