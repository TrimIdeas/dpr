What is the Bell X-14?

The Bell X-14 was a tilt-wing aircraft developed by Bell Aircraft Corporation in the late 1950s. It was one of the first experimental aircraft to successfully demonstrate vertical takeoff and landing (VTOL) capabilities, paving the way for modern helicopters and other VTOL aircraft.

Key Milestones

  • 1955: The Bell X-14 program begins as an experimental VTOL aircraft project.
  • 1957: First successful vertical takeoff.
  • 1960: Bell X-14 reaches top speed of 200 mph during test flights.
  • 1965: The project is retired, but its findings continue to influence aircraft design today.
